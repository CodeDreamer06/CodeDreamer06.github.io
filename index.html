§ 1 About

I'm 19, based in Hyderabad, India. I’m a software engineer, a student of philosophy, and a writer. I speak English, Hindi, and Telugu.

I don’t have a grand thesis for my life yet. Most days, I’m trying to become a little more precise: in thought, in language, in work, and in how I treat people.

College is still new to me, so I’m mostly learning how to balance ambition with patience. Classes, metro rides, books, long stretches of building things, and the occasional attempt at becoming less physically useless.

§ 2 Reading & Interests

I read philosophy and psychology to understand myself and the world a little better. Much of that comes from thinking about meaning, mortality, and how to make good use of limited time. My current interests include emotional intelligence, codependency, childhood’s imprint on personality, dream analysis, and frameworks for self-understanding.

I also enjoy chess, Greek mythology, and learning about how people structure deep work. I’ve played the piano for as long as I can remember; I'm trying to write novels, though that's very much a work in progress.

I like optimising small things: keyboard shortcuts, playback speed, how a workspace is laid out. Partly out of curiosity, partly because my spine reminds me to be kinder to my body.

§ 7 Principles

  • Friendships are exchanges of worldview. I look at people as evolving ideas.
  • Keep your sorrows in perspective. Nothing is ever so important that you must surrender your peace to it. The ultimatum of life is simply enjoying it.
  • Choose the story that requires effort. We pay the price of living either way, might as well live the version worth telling.
  • Relationships don’t need to last forever. it’s to help each other become who you’re meant to be, even if you eventually drift apart.
